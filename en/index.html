Method · five phases

From a complex idea to a story you remember

We treat every subject the way a director treats a film: select, simplify, narrate, design, then listen. Five phases, applied to every piece of content we publish.

01
Phase One · Selection

We choose the subject

One decision, one number, or one event worth understanding deeply · not just a passing headline that's consumed in a minute and forgotten in an hour.

  • CriterionImpact, not noise
  • OutputOne sharp angle
  • InputNumbers · facts · sources

02
Phase Two · Simplification

We simplify without reducing

We strip the jargon, never the idea. The reader leaves with the principle, not the vocabulary. Simplicity is respect for intelligence, not surrender of depth.

  • RuleEvery sentence earns its place
  • BannedFiller and fog
  • AllowedThe example, the analogy, the number

03
Phase Three · Narration

We tell it like a film

Characters, tension, transformation. The information arrives through the story, not before it. The reader doesn't memorize the report — they don't forget the scene.

  • StructureCause · event · consequence
  • PacingRising tension
  • ClimaxOne unforgettable turn

Phase Five · Publish & Listen

We publish, and listen

Content opens the conversation; it doesn't close it. We read every comment, and we shape the next angle from what the reader said · not from what we expected.
